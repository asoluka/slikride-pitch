01 / 09

The Problem

Two broken
sides of the
same road.

Riders outside Lagos have no reliable option.

Uber and Bolt don't exist in most Nigerian cities. People are left with informal, uncoordinated arrangements. No safety. No reliability.

Drivers on existing platforms feel abandoned.

Fuel hikes and inflation aren't reflected in fares. Platforms take 25%+ and don't listen. Drivers renegotiate offline — everyone loses.

Businesses have no last-mile delivery infrastructure.

Restaurants and vendors improvise with bike-men. No tracking, no reliability, no system.

02 / 09

The Founding Insight

A driver said something
that changed everything.

"If we could get a company that understands us, we'd leave Bolt."

— A BOLT DRIVER. LAGOS, 2024

Austin heard the same frustration from drivers in Lagos, Warri, Benin, and Port Harcourt. Riders confirmed it. Influencers complained publicly. This wasn't one bad experience — it was a systemic failure.

When he arrived in Ogbomoso — a city with no ride-hailing at all — he saw the opportunity clearly: build from where it's hardest, and it works everywhere.
